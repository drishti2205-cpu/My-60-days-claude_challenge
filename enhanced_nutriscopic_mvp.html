Nutrition Sources

Data provenance, methodology and references used by NutriScope.

Food Composition Data
1
NIN / ICMR Food Composition Tables
National Institute of Nutrition, India. "Nutritive Value of Indian Foods" (2017 edition). Primary source for all Indian foods including rice, roti, dal, paneer, idli, dosa, poha, chana, rajma, spinach and regional items.
2
USDA FoodData Central
U.S. Department of Agriculture. FoodData Central (2023). Used for internationally common foods: eggs, chicken, fish, oats, bread, milk, fruits, nuts and seeds. fdc.nal.usda.gov
3
IFCT 2017 — Indian Food Composition Tables
Compiled by NIN, Hyderabad. Extended micronutrient data including zinc, magnesium, potassium, folate, vitamin A and vitamin K for Indian foods.
4
FAO/WHO Food Energy Methods
FAO Food and Nutrition Paper 77. Atwater factors used for energy calculation: Protein 4 kcal/g, Carbohydrate 4 kcal/g, Fat 9 kcal/g.
Targets & RDAs
5
ICMR-NIN RDA 2020
Indian Council of Medical Research. "Nutrient Requirements and Recommended Dietary Allowances for Indians" (2020). Basis for all macro and micronutrient targets used in this app.
6
Harris-Benedict Equation (revised)
Roza AM, Shizgal HM (1984). "The Harris Benedict equation reevaluated." Am J Clin Nutr. Used to calculate Basal Metabolic Rate. Activity multipliers from McArdle, Katch & Katch (2010).
Methodology Notes
How Targets Are Calculated
  • TDEE: BMR × activity factor, adjusted for goal (lose/gain/muscle)
  • Protein: 1.6 g/kg (veg) · 1.8 g/kg (nonveg) · 2.0 g/kg (muscle goal)
  • Fat: 25% of TDEE ÷ 9
  • Carbs: Remaining kcal ÷ 4
  • Iron: 18 mg (women <50) · 8 mg (others) · 27 mg (pregnancy)
  • Calcium: 1000 mg (adults) · 1200 mg (women >50 / pregnancy)
  • Vitamin D: 15 µg/day (all adults per ICMR 2020)
  • Folate: 400 µg/day · 600 µg (pregnancy)
  • Zinc: 11 mg (men) · 8 mg (women)
  • Magnesium: 400 mg (men) · 310 mg (women)
  • Potassium: 3500 mg/day (WHO 2012)
Important Limitations
  • Nutrient values are averages — cooking method, ripeness and variety affect actual content significantly
  • Bioavailability (absorption) differs by food combinations (e.g. iron from plant foods is less absorbed)
  • BMI is a population-level tool and may not accurately reflect individual body composition
  • Risk scores are heuristic, not clinically validated models
  • This tool does not account for supplementation, medications, or individual metabolic variation
